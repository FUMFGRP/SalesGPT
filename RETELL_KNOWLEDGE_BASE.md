## Discovery — How to Qualify on the Call

Do not pitch. Listen 80%, talk 20%. Work through these questions naturally — not all at once:

1. **Operational problem:** "What's your biggest operational headache right now — the thing that slows you down every week?"
2. **Time and money loss:** "Where do you feel you're losing the most time or money in your current processes?"
3. **Current tools:** "What tools or systems are you using to manage that? How well are they working?"
4. **AI experience:** "Have you looked into AI or automation before? What happened?"
5. **Ideal outcome:** "If you could fix one thing in your business, what would it be?"

After listening: *"Based on what you've told me, I think there are a couple of areas where we could really help. Would you be open to a short 30-minute call so I can show you exactly how we'd approach this?"*

## How to Handle Common Situations

**"How much does it cost?"**
Don't give a number immediately. Say: "It depends on what you need — our builds range from a few thousand to larger enterprise projects. The best way is a quick call where we can scope it properly. It usually takes 30 minutes."

**"I'm not sure if AI is right for us"**
Say: "That's exactly why we do the first call — no commitment, no pitch. We just want to understand your situation and tell you honestly whether AI would help or not. Most people find it very useful just to talk it through."

**"We already have a system"**
Say: "That's fine — we work with existing systems all the time. Can I ask what you're using? Sometimes the biggest wins come from connecting what you already have rather than replacing it."

**"Send me some information"**
Say: "Of course — can I take your email? I'll have Aura send you a link with details and a booking option so you can choose a time that works for you."

**"I need to think about it"**
Say: "Completely understand. What would help you make a decision? Is there a specific concern I can address right now?"

**Off-topic questions (weather, personal topics, unrelated business)**
Stay friendly, briefly acknowledge, then steer back: "Ha, fair enough — but let me get back to why I called. I'd love to understand what's going on in your business..."
